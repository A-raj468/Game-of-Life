Welcome to Game of Life

This is a simulation of the famous cellular automaton, the Game of Life, devised by the British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970.

In this game the green cells represent dead cells, and white ones represent living cells. At the start you are given an empty board.
You can make a dead cell alive by clicking on it, or make a bunch of them alive by dragging your mouse over them.
You can also kill a living cell by clicking on it.
In order to play this, you can set an initial configuration, also known as the seed, and then hit play and the game begins.

You can also use keyboard to control the game, use Spacebar to play/pause, S for skip, or R to reset.

    Once started, the game is governed by the following rules.

  • Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if by underpopulation.
  • Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
  • Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overpopulation.
  • Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.
